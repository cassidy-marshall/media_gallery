Concert Photography

Concert Photography can be hard to shoot. You need to get the ISO just right while maintaining a high shutter speed to capture all of the action happening on stage.

In order to make sure your shots come out as clear as possible, play around with the ISO and appature until you like the looks of the shots. You can always fix lighting in post edits. To be safe I always suggest shooting some in auto to have as a backup.

At the Beach

Shooting at the beach can be challenging for multiple reasons. The first of which is the crowds of people, in order to combat this, I suggest shooting in the early morning when there are less people.

ALso, keep an eye on the weather forcast. A cludy day means less people, and sometimes unique shots of the usual sunny beach.

Drone Shots

Shooting with a drone is something that every photographer should try. Whether at a concert, or at the beach, a drone can provide a unique prespective.

The most important thing to learn before shooting with a drone is how to fly it. Take some time to learn the controls and operating the camera setting before taking up over a beach or rocky mountain top.

Black & White

Whether you are shooting in film, or doing it all in post editing, black and white photography is an easy way to add dramatic and timeless class to a portfolio and photoshoot.

My favorite tool to use is adobe photoshop and lightroom to adjust the gray scale. Juct because the photo is lacking color, does not mean you can't change the mood.
